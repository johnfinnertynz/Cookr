# Cookr MVP Product Requirements

## Problem
Many New Zealanders default to Uber Eats or other delivery because choosing a meal, shopping, cooking terms, timing, and cleanup all feel like separate sources of friction.

## Primary User
Beginner or low-confidence NZ cooks who want to save money, reduce takeaways, and cook simple meals with supermarket-friendly ingredients.

## MVP Goals
- Recommend meals based on confidence, time, budget, goals, dietary needs, appliances, and disliked ingredients.
- Convert selected recipes into a merged NZ grocery list.
- Provide Woolworths NZ product/search links without scraping result pages or automating checkout.
- Guide cooking step by step with plain-English help, substitutions, timers, and panic support.
- Feel like a polished consumer app rather than a recipe database.

## MVP Scope
- First-use onboarding.
- Seeded manually curated recipe catalogue with source/licensing notes.
- Recipe filtering and recommendation scoring.
- Recipe detail pages with easier-version guidance.
- Weekly plan preview, favourites, repeat-meal affordances, leftovers mode.
- Grocery merge, pantry-staple separation, estimated basket total.
- Woolworths assistant links and manual curated product matches.
- Cooking mode with scaled servings, prep checklist, cooking terms, substitutions, and beginner help.
- Dark mode via system preference and mobile-first responsive layout.

## Non-Goals
- No checkout integration.
- No scraping Woolworths product result pages.
- No use of copyrighted recipe text from third-party publishers.
- No production auth, payments, or nutrition-grade medical claims in MVP.

## Success Metrics
- User can complete onboarding in under 2 minutes.
- User can pick 2 meals and generate a shopping list in under 90 seconds.
- At least 80% of seed ingredients produce a high or medium confidence product/search match.
- Cooking mode keeps one instruction visible and readable on a mobile screen.
